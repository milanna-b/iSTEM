Our Strands

Web Application Development & Programming

The Web App Development and Programming strand goes through the fundamentals of computer programming and software development, with a specialized focus on web-based applications. Over the course of four years, students delve into various programming languages and concepts, progressively building their skills and expertise.

Year 1: Foundations of Programming

Students are introduced to the core concepts of programming through JavaScript. They learn the basics of HTML/CSS for coding fundamentals and explore object-oriented programming. Additionally, they focus on developing essential career-oriented skills and work towards earning the ITS JavaScript Certification.

Year 2: Procedural Programming

Building upon their foundational knowledge, students advance to procedural programming using Python. They engage in interactive coding projects, develop a digital portfolio, and earn the ITS Python Certification.

Year 3: Object-Oriented Program Fundamentals

This year emphasizes object-oriented programming principles, with a focus on utilizing HTML/CSS for script programming within a Visual Studio IDE. Students work towards earning certifications in HTML/CSS and HTML 5 App Development Fundamentals.

Year 4: JavaScript Programming

In the final year, students deepen their understanding of JavaScript programming. They explore block-based coding and engage in extensive programming projects, further refining their skills in web app development.

Biotechnology

What is Biotechnology?

Biotechnology is a field that harnesses living organisms or their components—such as DNA, proteins, or cells—to develop new products or processes that benefit society. It merges biology with technology, allowing scientists to manipulate biological systems for various applications. These applications span a wide range of industries, including agriculture, medicine, pharmaceuticals, environmental science, and more. Biotechnology enables researchers to engineer living organisms or utilize their biological processes to solve problems, improve existing products, or create entirely new ones.

Curriculum Highlights

  • Rigorous 4-year academic program emphasizing STEM subjects like advanced mathematics, science, research, and engineering technology.
  • Biotechnology I, II, III, IV courses.
  • Access to state-of-the-art biotechnology labs.
  • Field trips to biotechnology companies.
  • Guest speakers.
  • Opportunity to take the Biotechnician Assistant Credential Exam (BACE).

Computer Systems & Information Technology (CSIT)

The Computer Systems and Information Technology (CSIT) strand equips students with the skills and knowledge necessary for successful employment or further training within the information technology industry. The primary purpose of this program is to provide students with a strong foundation in various aspects of IT, emphasizing broad, transferable skills essential for success in this field. Students will gain proficiency in technical and product skills, understand the underlying principles of technology, and develop competencies in planning, management, finance, and other critical areas relevant to the IT industry.

Key Objectives

  • Exploring essential IT concepts, terminology, and components.
  • Understanding computer hardware, software, and operating systems.
  • Gaining knowledge about networking fundamentals and connectivity.
  • Learning about data storage, security, and privacy best practices.
  • Acquiring hands-on experience with basic troubleshooting and problem-solving.
  • Preparing for further IT certifications and career advancement opportunities.

The course curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including communication, leadership skills, human relations, employability skills, and safe work practices. Moreover, students will engage in hands-on projects aimed at building practical skills, such as building computers, optimizing performance, water cooling, virtual reality, augmented reality, custom gaming simulators, and drones.

Cybersecurity

The Cybersecurity strand is designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills needed to thrive in the rapidly evolving field of cybersecurity. Throughout the course, students will learn about various topics, including:

  • Computer & Network Security Fundamentals
  • Windows and Linux OS Fundamentals
  • Malware & Social Engineering Attacks/Defenses
  • Network & Application Attacks/Defenses
  • Host, Application, and Data Security
  • Cryptography & Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)
  • Wired and Wireless Network Security
  • Mobile Device & Embedded Device Security
  • IoT & Cloud Security Fundamentals
  • Authentication & Access Control Management
  • Business Continuity Practices
  • Risk Management and Mitigation Practices
  • Vulnerability Assessment & Basic Penetration Testing
  • Incident Response & Forensic Analysis

Moreover, the program offers industry certification opportunities, including CompTIA Network+, CompTIA Security+, and CompTIA CySA+, among others. These certifications provide students with valuable credentials and open doors to various career paths in IT networking and cybersecurity.

Digital Design

The Digital Design strand offers a curriculum focused on digital publishing and design, catering to both career-oriented individuals and enthusiasts alike.
Throughout the program, students gain hands-on experience with a wide range of Adobe software, including Photoshop, InDesign, Illustrator, Premiere Pro, and After Effects. Instructor-led lessons provide a structured learning environment, supplemented by projects tailored to students' interests and relevant to current events. Moreover, the program offers Adobe industry certifications at every level, ensuring students' proficiency and enhancing their employability.
By the end of the program, students possess a deep understanding of digital design principles and practices, along with a portfolio showcasing their proficiency in various digital media formats. With a 96% pass rate in Adobe certifications during the 2022-2023 school year, the Digital Design strand equips students with the skills and certifications necessary to thrive in today's digital publishing industry.

Engineering

The Engineering strand of the Building Trades and Construction Design Technology program focuses on preparing students for employment or further training in the building construction industry. Throughout the course, students gain competencies in various aspects of construction, safety practices, and employability skills.
In Building Trades and Construction Design Technology 1, students learn fundamental safety practices, the use of hand and power tools, construction components, materials, and hardware. They also explore construction industry occupations and develop employability skills.
Building Trades and Construction Design Technology 2 delves deeper into specific skills such as rough and finish carpentry, masonry, and painting. Students learn to construct and install various structural elements, apply finishes, and understand building science principles.
In Building Trades and Construction Design Technology 3, students further develop their competencies in construction-related math and science, explore the built environment, and understand the principles of sustainable and green construction. They investigate new technologies, analyze data, and discuss the impact of construction on both the natural and built environments.
Throughout the program, students engage in hands-on learning experiences, including practical exercises, project-based assignments, and industry-related simulations. By the end of the course, students are equipped with the skills and knowledge necessary for successful entry into the building construction industry or advanced training in related fields.

Game & Simulation

The Game & Simulation strand prepares students for careers in the field of game and simulation development within the Information Technology career cluster. This sequence of courses aligns with academic standards while providing students with relevant technical knowledge and skills.
Throughout the program, students have the opportunity to earn various industry certifications, including Unity Certified User: Programmer, Unity Certified User: VR Developer, and Oracle Foundations Associate: Java, among others. These certifications validate their proficiency in key areas of game and simulation development, enhancing their employability in the field.

Year 1: Foundations

Students gain a foundational understanding of the game design process, explore vocational opportunities, and learn 2D art and animation using tools like Scratch, Construct3, and Unity. Additionally, they acquire proficiency in using Microsoft products for reporting purposes.

Year 2: Design

Building on this foundation, students delve deeper into the game design process, focusing on 2D design while being introduced to 3D art and animation tools such as Blender. They also begin working with 3D game engines like Unity.

Years 3-4: Programming & Multi-User Simulation

Students advance their knowledge and skills, deepening their understanding of 3D art and animation tools and 3D game engines, with a focus on building and testing 3D single and multiplayer games for various platforms. Moreover, students explore backend server technology for implementing features like leaderboards and game economy and have the opportunity to work on Virtual Reality (VR) game development projects.
